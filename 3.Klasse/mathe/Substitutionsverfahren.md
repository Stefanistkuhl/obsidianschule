### Substitutionsverfahren
$$\int    e^{\sin x} \cos x \, dx$$
$$= \int e^u = \int e^u \, du = e^u +e = e^{\sin x}+c $$
#### Substituieren
$$u=\sin x$$
$$\frac{du}{dx}=\cos x$$
$$dx = \frac{dx}{\cos x}$$
##### Probe
$$(e^{\sin x}+c)'= e^{\sin x}*\cos x$$
Bsp:
$$\int \sin x^3 *x^2 \, dx $$
$$=\int \frac{1}{3} \sin(u) \, du = -\frac{1}{3}\cos u +c$$
$$=-\frac{1}{3}\cos(x^3)+c$$
$$u=x^3$$
$$\frac{du}{dx}=3x^2$$
$$dx = \frac{du}{2x^2}$$
